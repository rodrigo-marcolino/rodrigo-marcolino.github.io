Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Learn about neuroplasticity and growth mindset was a watershed in my journey as a student. Now I am sure that our intelligence and talents can be developed over time. There is a ton of research on the topic already.

However, I must admit that I Had a Fixed Mindset and looking back now I think about how my life could be different if I knew about that before.

Comparing how I was then, to how I am now.

Fixed Mindset

Growth Mindset

I Believed that intelligence, talents, and other abilities are fixed
I Believe that intelligence can be improved and Embracing lifelong learning
I Avoided challenges to prevent the possibility of failure
I Believe that failures are just temporary setbacks
I hid the flaws so I wouldn't be judged by others
I Willingly embrace challenges
I gave up easily
I Put in more effort to learn

I have a negative inner voice that acts against a growth mindset. Now I am Trying to flip thoughts such as ‘I can’t do this’, to ‘I can do this if I keep practicing’ to nurture a growth mindset.

I am learning how hard work, good strategies, and good use of resources lead to better learning.

“The passion for stretching yourself and sticking to it, even (or especially) when it’s not going well, is the hallmark of the growth mindset.”

Carol S. Dweck
